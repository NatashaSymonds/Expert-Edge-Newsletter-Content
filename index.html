An interview with

Paul Alexander

Managing Director, Expert360 New Zealand
As Managing Director of Expert360 New Zealand, Paul leads the NZ business across all divisions — bringing together clients, Experts and the broader Expert360 team to deliver exceptional outcomes. With AI now central to how Expert360 operates and evolves, we sat down with Paul to hear his full, unedited perspective on what it means for the business, and for the Experts in our community.
What was your own "aha moment" with AI?

My "aha moment" was when I stopped treating AI like a search engine and started treating it like a very capable first-draft partner.

The first time I used it properly to turn messy thoughts, meeting notes, commercial context and half-formed ideas into something structured and useful, I realised this was not just another tool. It was a genuine change in how work gets done.

For me, the shift was from "AI gives answers" to "AI helps me think, prepare, communicate and move faster". That was the moment it clicked. But that was quite a while ago now!

How has your own day-to-day changed since E360 started embedding AI tools?

The biggest change is speed to first draft.

I use AI constantly now to help structure thinking, prepare for meetings, sharpen client messages, summarise complex topics, draft plans, stress-test ideas and turn conversations into actions.

It has not replaced judgement... but it has absolutely reduced the blank-page problem. I can get to a useful starting point much faster, then spend my time improving, challenging and commercialising the output.

In practical terms, I am doing more of the thinking work earlier, with better structure, and with less admin drag.

Paul, in plain terms — where is E360 on its AI journey right now?

We are past the "is this real?" stage.

We are now in the "how do we embed this properly?" stage.

That means using AI in practical, everyday ways across our business — not as a gimmick, but as a productivity and quality uplift. We are looking at how AI can support better intake, better matching, better communication, faster shortlists, stronger proposals, better account planning and better use of the knowledge we already have across the business.

We are still learning. Everyone is. But the direction is clear. AI will increasingly become part of how high-performing people and teams work.

"We are past the 'is this real?' stage. We are now in the 'how do we embed this properly?' stage."
— PAUL ALEXANDER
What does that look like practically for our Expert community?

For Experts, I think the opportunity is very real.

Clients are already asking how to do more with less, how to move faster, how to improve service delivery, and how to use AI safely and practically. Experts who can combine strong domain experience with practical AI literacy will be extremely valuable.

That does not mean every Expert needs to become a technologist. It means understanding how AI can help in your area of expertise.

For example:

  • A project manager using AI to improve planning, reporting and risk tracking.
  • A business analyst using AI to accelerate discovery, documentation and synthesis.
  • A change manager using AI to tailor comms, training and stakeholder material.
  • A consultant using AI to build sharper options papers and recommendations.
  • A delivery lead using AI to improve cadence, visibility and decision-making.

The Experts who lean in will have an advantage. Not because AI replaces their expertise... but because it amplifies it.

What's your message to Experts who are looking to start their AI journey?

Start small. Start today. Do not wait until you feel like an expert.

Pick one part of your work that is repetitive, slow or annoying, and use AI to help with that. It might be drafting meeting notes, preparing interview questions, summarising a document, creating a stakeholder update, building a first-cut plan or pressure-testing a recommendation.

The key is to keep the human in the loop. AI should not be blindly trusted, and it should not be used carelessly with sensitive information. But used well, it can be an incredible assistant.

My advice is simple... do not make AI a theoretical topic. Make it a daily habit.

Any exciting things on the horizon you can share?

The exciting thing for me is not one single tool. It is the way AI starts connecting the whole Expert360 model.

Better understanding of client needs. Faster identification of the right Experts. Better matching of skills to outcomes. Stronger proposals. More useful market insight. More efficient delivery support. Better visibility across the work we are already doing.

The long-term opportunity is to make the experience better for everyone... clients, Experts and our internal teams.

For Experts, that should mean better visibility of relevant opportunities, better use of their capability and a more efficient way of connecting the right people to the right work.

What's the one AI tool or habit you'd recommend every Expert tries before the end of 2026?

The habit I would recommend is building your own AI "prep partner".

Before any important meeting, workshop, interview, proposal or client conversation, use AI to help you prepare.

Ask it to challenge your thinking. Ask it what questions you should ask. Ask it what risks you may have missed. Ask it to summarise the audience, the likely objections and the best way to explain your recommendation in plain English.

That one habit alone can make people sharper, faster and more confident.

The tool matters less than the behaviour. The real unlock is learning how to ask better questions.

"Don't make AI a theoretical topic. Make it a daily habit."
— PAUL ALEXANDER, MANAGING DIRECTOR, EXPERT360 NEW ZEALAND
