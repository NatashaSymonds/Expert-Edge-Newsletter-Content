02 · Inside Expert360
An interview with
Paul Alexander
Managing Director, Expert360 New Zealand

As Managing Director of Expert360 New Zealand, Paul leads the NZ business across all divisions. With AI now central to how Expert360 operates and evolves, we sat down with Paul to hear his perspective on what it means for the business — and for the Experts in our community.

What was your own "aha moment" with AI?

My "aha moment" was when I stopped treating AI like a search engine and started treating it like a very capable first-draft partner.

The first time I used it properly — turning messy thoughts, meeting notes, commercial context and half-formed ideas into something structured and useful — I realised this was not just another tool. It was a genuine change in how work gets done.

For me, the shift was from "AI gives answers" to "AI helps me think, prepare, communicate and move faster." That was the moment it clicked.

"Don't make AI a theoretical topic. Make it a daily habit."
— Paul Alexander, Managing Director, Expert360 New Zealand

05 · Feature article
AI & the NZ Job Market — What It Means for Your Career

The government has announced plans to cut approximately 9,000 public service roles by 2029, with AI explicitly named as part of the strategy.

The question isn't whether AI will reshape the workforce — it already is. The real question is how you position yourself in that shift.

"The professionals who will thrive aren't the ones who fear AI — they're the ones who learn to work alongside it."

Thoughts on AI in your sector? Hit reply and tell us.

How to stay ahead
01
Start using AI in your day-to-day
Pick one repetitive part of your work — meeting prep, drafting comms, summarising documents — and use AI to help. Small daily habits build real fluency faster than any course.
02
Your experience is your edge
Clients aren't looking for pure AI specialists — they're looking for experienced professionals who understand how AI applies in their sector. Domain expertise plus AI literacy is the winning combination.
03
Build skills that AI can't replace
Strategic thinking, stakeholder management, judgement, and communication remain deeply human. Keep sharpening these alongside your technical capability — they are what clients will pay a premium for.

Key trends this quarter
●
Talent shortages persist
The market remains candidate-short across specialist skill sets. Quality of applications has declined, making it harder to secure top-tier talent quickly.
▲
Counteroffers on the rise
Employers are working harder to retain valued staff, contributing to longer time-to-fill and higher drop-off rates at final stages.
↔
Shifting candidate preferences
Permanent employees are increasingly open to contract roles. Some long-term contractors are gravitating toward permanent positions. Notice periods remain a friction point for urgent contract needs.
■
Growth in longer-term contracts
Roles extending through to 2027 are boosting confidence. Many short-term engagements have extended to 12 months, reflecting growing confidence in delivery roadmaps.
▼
Budget pressure & value scrutiny
Many customers await FY27 budget confirmation. Procurement teams are challenging costs, but the volume of discussions suggests a healthy pipeline building for H2.

07 · Reminders & Prompts
Operational Reminders
Q3 school holidays & public holidays
A reminder of key dates coming up in Q3. Please ensure you have notified your ERM of any planned leave in advance, and confirmed your approver will be available to approve timesheets while you are away.
School Holidays — 6–19 July.
Matariki — Thursday 10 July (public holiday).
End of Term 3 — Friday 26 September. If you have school-aged children, now is a good time to plan ahead for the October school holidays.
Weekly timesheets
Submit by COB Friday each week. Approvals completed weekly where possible. If you are on leave, ensure your approver is aware and able to cover.
Month-end timesheets
Timesheets must be submitted by 3:00pm on the last working day of the month, with approvals completed by COB on the first working day of the new month. Two month-ends this quarter fall mid-week — you will need to submit two separate timesheets for each of these months: one to close off the current month, and a second for the first few days of the next month.
August — Monday 31 August is the last working day. Submit by 3:00pm Monday 31 August.
September — Wednesday 30 September is the last working day. Submit by 3:00pm Wednesday 30 September.
Planning leave through to Christmas?
The second half of the year tends to move fast — with October school holidays, Labour Day, and the Christmas/New Year period all coming up. If you are planning any leave between now and the end of the year, please give your ERM as much notice as possible. Early communication helps us plan ahead with your client and ensure a smooth handover where needed. The more notice the better — we appreciate it!
